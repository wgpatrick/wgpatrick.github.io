The Moonshot

A Self-Driving Biologics Factory

Need

Biologics production volume1 must double in the next 10 years to keep up with demand

1 Specifically, mammalian cell culture production, which is the leading method for producing biologic therapeutics.

Problem

Factories are expensive ($500M–$1B) and labor intensive to operate.

Substantial cleanroom layouts

Trained technicians required

Solution

A self-driving factory can scale to meet global needs

Eliminate clean rooms

Site anywhere

Reduce opex

Reduce lost batches

Solution

Eliminating Grade C clean rooms can reduce construction hard costs by >40%

Solution

Much cheaper biologics production

0%
×

Assumptions

  • Fed-batch monoclonal antibody (mAb) production process
  • Utilization: 70%
  • Total baseline COGS: $60/g
  • Raw Materials: $10.8/g (18%)
  • Labor: $9.0/g (15%)
  • QA/QC: $4.2/g (7%)
  • Utilities: $1.8/g (3%)
  • Facility Depreciation: $15.4/g (25.7%)
  • Equipment Depreciation: $12.0/g (20.0%)
  • Facility MRO: $6.8/g (11.3%)
  • Low-cost site saves 20% on labor, facility depreciation, and facility MRO; 10% on utilities. Equipment depreciation unaffected.
  • Facility cost slider only affects facility depreciation, not equipment.
  • MRO costs fall half as steeply with facility cost reduction.
  • Model based on: Monoclonal antibody production cost analysis
